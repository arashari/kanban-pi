NASI GORENG (Indonesian Fried Rice)

Serves: 2
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 10 minutes

INGREDIENTS:
- 2 cups cooked jasmine rice (day-old, chilled)
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 shallot, finely chopped
- 1-2 red chilies, sliced (optional)
- 2 tbsp kecap manis (sweet soy sauce)
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp shrimp paste (terasi) or fish sauce
- 2 eggs
- 100g chicken breast, diced (or shrimp/tofu)
- 4-5 prawns, peeled (optional)
- Salt and pepper to taste

For serving:
- Fried shallots
- Sliced cucumber
- Tomato wedges
- Prawn crackers (kerupuk)
- Pickled vegetables (acar)
- Extra kecap manis

INSTRUCTIONS:
1. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a wok over high heat. Scramble the eggs, then remove and set aside.

2. Add remaining oil. Stir-fry garlic, shallot, and chilies for 30 seconds until fragrant.

3. Add chicken (and prawns if using). Cook until chicken is done and prawns turn pink, about 3-4 minutes.

4. Add the cold rice. Break up any clumps with your spatula. Fry for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly.

5. Stir in kecap manis, soy sauce, and shrimp paste. Mix well until rice is evenly coated and slightly caramelized.

6. Add the scrambled eggs back in. Season with salt and pepper. Toss everything together for another minute.

7. Serve hot, garnished with fried shallots, cucumber, tomato, prawn crackers, and a drizzle of kecap manis.

TIPS:
- Cold, day-old rice works best as it's less sticky and fries better.
- Adjust kecap manis to taste — some like it sweeter, some prefer less.
- For a vegetarian version, skip the meat and shrimp, and use tofu or tempeh instead.

Selamat makan! (Enjoy your meal!)
